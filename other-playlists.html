The Very Best of Scriabin

Scriabin, the Russian composer from the 1900's (similar era to Tchaikovsky and Rachmaninoff), is often hailed as the greatest composer, for his ability to express emotions more deeply than any other composer. His style ranges from nice Chopin-inspired melodies to the filthiest chord progressions imaginable, in an attempt to transcend humanity to reach the ethereal plane. Seriously, have a look at this madman's Wikipedia page.

Best Rachmaninoff Etudes

The Rachmaninoff Etudes-Tableaux ("Study-Paintings") are the pinnacle of late Romantic piano music. They're some of the most technically demanding pieces ever written, and packed with complexity, but also have moments of pure beauty and emotion. They will definitely release some dopamine in your brain.
